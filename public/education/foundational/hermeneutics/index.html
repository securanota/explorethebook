Introduction to Biblical Hermeneutics

Introduction to Biblical Hermeneutics#

Course Description#

This course introduces students to the principles and practices of biblical interpretation, equipping them to read, understand, and apply Scripture faithfully.

Course Objectives#

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • [To be filled: List specific learning objectives]
  • Understand the basic principles of biblical interpretation
  • Apply grammatical-historical method to biblical texts
  • Recognize different literary genres in Scripture
  • Move from observation to interpretation to application
  • Avoid common interpretive errors

Prerequisites#

  • Basic familiarity with the Bible
  • Commitment to Scripture’s authority and truthfulness
  • Willingness to study diligently

Course Structure#

Week 1: Introduction to Hermeneutics#

  • Lesson 1: What is Biblical Hermeneutics?
  • Lesson 2: Why Proper Interpretation Matters
  • Assignment: Personal Interpretation Inventory

Week 2: The Nature of Scripture#

  • Lesson 1: Divine Inspiration and Human Authors
  • Lesson 2: Scripture’s Unity and Diversity
  • Assignment: Comparing Gospel Accounts

Week 3: Historical-Grammatical Method#

  • Lesson 1: Historical Context
  • Lesson 2: Grammatical Analysis
  • Assignment: Context Study of Philippians 4:13

Week 4: Literary Genres - Narrative#

  • Lesson 1: Understanding Biblical Narrative
  • Lesson 2: Interpreting Old Testament Stories
  • Assignment: Analysis of David and Goliath

Week 5: Literary Genres - Poetry and Wisdom#

  • Lesson 1: Hebrew Poetry Principles
  • Lesson 2: Wisdom Literature
  • Assignment: Psalm 23 Analysis

Week 6: Literary Genres - Prophecy#

  • Lesson 1: Understanding Prophetic Literature
  • Lesson 2: Messianic Prophecy
  • Assignment: Isaiah 53 Study

Week 7: New Testament Genres - Gospels#

  • Lesson 1: Gospel Genre and Purpose
  • Lesson 2: Parables and Sayings
  • Assignment: Parable Interpretation

Week 8: New Testament Genres - Epistles#

  • Lesson 1: Understanding Letters
  • Lesson 2: Occasional vs. General Epistles
  • Assignment: Romans 12:1-2 Exegesis

Week 9: Apocalyptic Literature#

  • Lesson 1: Characteristics of Apocalyptic
  • Lesson 2: Interpreting Revelation
  • Assignment: Revelation 2-3 Study

Week 10: Scripture Interprets Scripture#

  • Lesson 1: The Analogy of Faith
  • Lesson 2: Progressive Revelation
  • Assignment: Tracing a Theme Through Scripture

Week 11: From Interpretation to Application#

  • Lesson 1: The Hermeneutical Bridge
  • Lesson 2: Cultural vs. Universal Principles
  • Assignment: Application Study

Week 12: Common Errors and Final Project#

  • Lesson 1: Avoiding Interpretive Pitfalls
  • Lesson 2: Tools for Continued Study
  • Final Project: Complete Exegetical Study

Assessment Methods#

  • Weekly assignments (50%)
  • Midterm exam (20%)
  • Final project (25%)
  • Class participation (5%)
