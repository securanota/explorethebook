The Doctrine of God

The Doctrine of God (Theology Proper)#

Introduction#

The study of God Himself—His nature, attributes, and works—forms the foundation of all theological knowledge.

Core Articles in This Section#

God’s Nature and Being#

  • Divine Attributes - God’s perfections and qualities
  • Divine Simplicity - God’s unity and indivisibility
  • The Trinity - One God in three persons
  • Divine Immanence and Transcendence - God’s nearness and otherness

God’s Works#

  • Creation - God’s creative work and purpose
  • Providence - God’s sustaining and governing of creation
  • Divine Decrees - God’s eternal purposes and plans
  • Miracles - God’s extraordinary works in history

God’s Revelation#

  • General Revelation - God’s self-disclosure in creation and conscience
  • Special Revelation - God’s specific revelation in Scripture and Christ
  • The Names of God - How God reveals Himself through His names

Study Sequence#

For systematic study, we recommend this order:

  1. Divine Attributes (foundational understanding)
  2. Trinity (essential Christian doctrine)
  3. Creation (God’s first work)
  4. Providence (God’s ongoing work)
  5. General and Special Revelation (how we know God)

Contemporary Relevance#

Understanding God correctly affects:

  • Worship: Proper response to who God is
  • Prayer: Confidence in God’s character
  • Suffering: Trust in God’s sovereignty and goodness
  • Ethics: Living according to God’s nature
  • Evangelism: Proclaiming the true God

The Doctrine of God: Divine Attributes Introduction The study of God’s attributes forms the foundation of all theological reflection. Understanding who God is—His essential nature and perfections—shapes every other aspect of Christian doctrine and practice. Definition of Divine Attributes Divine attributes are the perfections or qualities that belong to God’s essential nature. They are not additions to God’s being but are identical with His essence.
