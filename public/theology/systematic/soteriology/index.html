The Doctrine of Salvation

The Doctrine of Salvation (Soteriology)#

Introduction#

Soteriology is the study of salvation—how God saves sinners and transforms them into His people.

Core Articles in This Section#

The Application of Salvation#

  • Election and Predestination - God’s sovereign choice
  • Calling - God’s summons to salvation
  • Regeneration - The new birth
  • Faith and Repentance - Human response to the gospel
  • Justification - Declared righteous before God
  • Adoption - Becoming children of God
  • Sanctification - Growth in holiness
  • Perseverance - Security of salvation
  • Glorification - Final transformation

Theological Debates#

  • Arminianism vs. Calvinism - Different views of salvation
  • Faith and Works - Role of good works in salvation
  • Eternal Security - Can salvation be lost?
  • Baptism - Sign and seal of salvation

Salvation History#

  • Covenant of Grace - God’s saving plan through history
  • Old Testament Salvation - How were people saved before Christ?
  • Universal Offer - Salvation available to all

Study Sequence#

For systematic study, we recommend this order:

  1. Election (God’s eternal purpose)
  2. Calling (bringing people to Christ)
  3. Regeneration (new spiritual life)
  4. Faith and Repentance (human response)
  5. Justification (legal standing with God)
  6. Sanctification (progressive transformation)
  7. Glorification (final perfection)
