The Doctrine of Scripture

The Doctrine of Scripture (Bibliology)#

Introduction#

The doctrine of Scripture addresses the nature, authority, and interpretation of God’s written Word.

Core Articles in This Section#

The Nature of Scripture#

  • Divine Inspiration - God’s role in producing Scripture
  • Inerrancy - Scripture’s freedom from error
  • Infallibility - Scripture’s complete trustworthiness
  • Authority - Scripture’s right to command belief and obedience
  • Sufficiency - Scripture’s adequacy for faith and practice
  • Clarity - Scripture’s essential perspicuity

The Formation of Scripture#

  • Canon - How the Bible came together
  • Old Testament Canon - Hebrew Scriptures
  • New Testament Canon - Apostolic writings
  • Apocrypha - Deuterocanonical books

Interpreting Scripture#

  • Hermeneutics - Principles of biblical interpretation
  • Grammatical-Historical Method - Orthodox approach to interpretation
  • Typology - Old Testament types and New Testament antitypes
  • Scripture Interprets Scripture - The analogy of faith

Contemporary Issues#

  • Translation Issues - From original languages to modern versions
  • Textual Criticism - Determining the original text
  • Archaeological Confirmation - External evidence for Scripture
  • Scripture and Science - Relating Bible and natural sciences

Study Sequence#

For foundational understanding, we recommend this order:

  1. Divine Inspiration (how we got Scripture)
  2. Authority (why Scripture commands belief)
  3. Inerrancy (Scripture’s truthfulness)
  4. Canon (which books belong)
  5. Hermeneutics (how to interpret)
